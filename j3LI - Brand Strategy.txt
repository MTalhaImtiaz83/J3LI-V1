**E1. Define your tone of voice in 4 attributes. For each: name, definition, 2 real DO sentences, 2 real DONT sentences.**
```
ANSWER:

TONE ATTRIBUTE 1
Name: Precise
Definition: Every word carries weight. No filler. No hedge words. If the data says it, say it clearly. If the data doesn't say it, say that clearly too. Precision is respect for the subject matter.
DO (2 real sentences in this tone):
    1. "This hadith is graded Sahih by Al-Albani and Hasan by Al-Arna'ut. Both gradings and their chains are shown below."
    2. "No authenticated scholarly source in our dataset directly links this verse to the Battle of Badr. The closest connection is through Surah Al-Anfal, which was revealed in its aftermath."
DONT (2 sentences that violate this tone):
    1. "Many scholars believe this hadith is probably authentic, and it seems to be related to this verse."
    2. "This verse might have been revealed around the time of an important battle."

TONE ATTRIBUTE 2
Name: Generous
Definition: Give more than asked. When someone searches for one thing, show them the web of connections around it. The brand teaches by revealing, not by lecturing.
DO:
    1. "You searched for Ayat al-Kursi (2:255). Here is the verse, 7 tafsir interpretations, 4 connected hadith about its virtues, and the root meanings of its 3 key Arabic terms."
    2. "The root ك-ت-ب appears 319 times in the Quran across 12 derived forms. Here are all of them, grouped by meaning."
DONT:
    1. "Here is the translation of 2:255."
    2. "The word 'kitab' means 'book.'"

TONE ATTRIBUTE 3
Name: Reverent Without Being Reverential
Definition: The brand respects the sacred nature of the content without performing reverence. No excessive salutations in UI copy.     1. "The Most Noble and Blessed Prophet (Peace and Blessings of Allah Be Upon Him) in His infinite wisdom taught us..."
