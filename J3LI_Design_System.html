Brand Essence

The Quran is not a flat text. It is an interconnected web of meaning spanning scripture, history, language, and 1,400 years of scholarship. Just Three Like It makes that web visible, navigable, and trustworthy for the first time.

The name references the Quranic challenge (al-Tahaddi). The smallest surah, Al-Kawthar, is three verses long. In 1,400 years, the challenge stands unmet. The system shows you why.

Personality Dimensions

Dimension 1

Scholarly Precision

Every word earns its place. Every claim has a source. Every citation is verifiable. Precision because the subject demands it.

Dimension 2

Quiet Boldness

The name is a challenge. The brand does not shout. The confidence comes from the work, not from the marketing.

Dimension 3

Generous Depth

Ask about one verse and see the hadith, the event, the scholarly debate, and the classical Arabic root. The depth is the gift.

Core Emotions

EmotionDescription
Awe at DepthA verse read a hundred times suddenly reveals layers never seen. Genuine intellectual awe when connections become visible.
Trust in Every ClaimQuiet confidence from seeing citations, grades, and sources on every statement. Earned trust through transparency.
Intellectual HungerThe pull to click the next node, trace the next chain, explore the next root. A beautiful rabbit hole of authenticated knowledge.
Respect for TraditionThe system amplifies 1,400 years of scholarship rather than bypassing it. Every scholar is credited.
Quiet EmpowermentA key to a library you didn't know you had access to. Empowerment through the tool, not dependency on it.

Ethical Non-Negotiables

  • Never fabricate a relationship between a Hadith and a Quran verse that does not exist in authenticated scholarly sources.
  • Never present a Da'if (weak) hadith as evidence without stating its grade and grading scholar.
  • Never use AI to generate Islamic rulings, fatwas, or theological opinions.
  • Never market using fear, guilt, or religious manipulation.
  • Never compromise source authentication for user engagement.

02

Voice & Verbal Identity

Tone System

Precise

Every word carries weight. No filler. No hedge words. If the data says it, say it clearly. If it doesn't, say that clearly too.

Do

This hadith is graded Sahih by Al-Albani and Hasan by Al-Arna'ut. Both gradings and their chains are shown below.

Don't

Many scholars believe this hadith is probably authentic, and it seems to be related to this verse.

Generous

Give more than asked. Show the web of connections around every search. Teach by revealing, not lecturing.

Do

You searched for Ayat al-Kursi (2:255). Here is the verse, 7 tafsir interpretations, 4 connected hadith, and the root meanings of its 3 key Arabic terms.

Don't

Here is the translation of 2:255.

Reverent Without Being Reverential

Respect the sacred nature of the content without performing reverence. The respect is in accuracy and depth.

Do

The Prophet Muhammad instructed his companions to recite this surah before sleeping. This narration appears in Sahih Al-Bukhari, hadith 5010.

Don't

The Most Noble and Blessed Prophet (Peace and Blessings of Allah Be Upon Him) in His infinite wisdom taught us...

Inviting

Pull the reader in. Hint at more depth than expected. The first sentence should make you want the second.

Do

Surah Al-Kawthar is three verses long. It is also the surah scholars have written more words about, per verse, than almost any other. Here's why.

Don't

Welcome to our Quranic research platform. Please use the search bar to find verses.

06

Motion Design System

Motion Principles

Motion serves meaning. The graph explorer's vocabulary — nodes expanding, connections tracing, depth revealing — is the brand's motion signature.

Reveals connections

Connected nodes animate outward. Motion shows the system finding relationships.

Communicates depth

Layers emerge with staggered timing. Deepest information arrives last.

Respects the content

Quranic text never animates character-by-character. It appears complete.

Is earned

First visit: full animations. Return visits: reduced. The system remembers.

Logo System

Logo Concept

The numeral "3" is the logo's geometric identity. Three verses, three-ness as the irreducible unit of the challenge. The "3" is the visual anchor — the first thing the eye finds.

Primary Mark

"JUST THREE LIKE IT" in Outfit 700, with the "3" at 150% scale in Dhahab gold. The rest in Bahri navy or reversed white.

Icon Mark

Isolated "3" for favicon, app icon, small-scale. Gold on navy, or navy on white. At 16px, only the 3 appears.

Arabic Wordmark

فَأْتُوا بِسُورَةٍ مِّن مِّثْلِهِ

Set in Amiri. Not a translation — the Quranic source. Both lockups are primary, equal in weight and authority.

08

Imagery & Visual Direction

Visual Language

The visual language is the graph itself — nodes, connections, depth, illumination. Arabic calligraphy in Amiri is the visual hero when the graph is not present. A verse rendered with care IS the image.

Prohibited Imagery

  • No generic Islamic imagery (mosque silhouettes, crescent moons, stereotypical patterns)
  • No stock photography of posed "diverse Muslims"
  • No Quranic text as decorative backgrounds or watermarks
  • No depiction of prophets, angels, or companions
  • No music in audio/video — ambient sound or silence only
  • No Tech Bro Gradient (purple-blue-teal) — no exceptions

12

Brand Behaviors

Always

  • Cite the source on every claim
  • State the authenticity grade for every hadith
  • Present multiple scholarly opinions when they exist
  • Use classical Arabic definitions from Lane's Lexicon
  • Say "no direct scholarly link found" when data doesn't support a claim

Never

  • Use startup hype vocabulary
  • Imply the system replaces scholars
  • State Shariah compliance as marketing
  • Use religious guilt or fear as engagement
  • Present AI synthesis as scholarly consensus
